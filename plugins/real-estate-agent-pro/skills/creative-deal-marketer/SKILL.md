When a seller agrees to offer creative financing terms, you face a critical challenge: the MLS is designed for traditional transactions. Creative deals require a completely different marketing channel strategy. This skill is your playbook for marketing non-traditional deals — finding the right buyers, crafting the right message, and tapping into the right communities.

## STEP 1: DEAL INTAKE

Collect or parse from the agent:

- **Property address and brief description**
- **Creative structure** being offered (seller finance, subject-to, lease-option, wrap mortgage, or combination)
- **Key terms**: purchase price, down payment, interest rate, monthly payment, balloon (if any), lease rate + option price
- **Property condition**: turnkey / light rehab / heavy rehab
- **Target buyer profile**: owner-occupant, investor, fix-and-flip, buy-and-hold landlord, first-time buyer who can't qualify for bank loan

---

## STEP 2: BUYER PROFILE MATCHING

Match the creative structure to the most likely buyer type and explain WHY:

**Subject-To** → Experienced real estate investors (manage existing loans, understand due-on-sale risk, want to build rental portfolio without new financing)

**Seller Finance (low down, low rate)** → Buyers who have income but can't qualify conventionally: self-employed, recent credit events, foreign nationals, high debt-to-income ratio

**Seller Finance (standard terms)** → Both investors (cash flow play) and owner-occupants (access to homeownership they couldn't otherwise achieve)

**Lease-Option** → Owner-occupant buyers who need 1-2 years to clean up credit; also works for investors doing sandwich lease-options

**Wrap Mortgage** → Sophisticated investors who want positive arbitrage between the underlying rate and the new rate they're charging

## STEP 3: MARKETING CHANNELS — THE FULL PLAYBOOK

### A. Online Communities & Marketplaces (Highest ROI for Creative Deals)

**Sub2/Creative Finance Communities:**
- Pace Morby's Sub2 Facebook Group (search: "Sub2 Deals") — largest creative finance community; post deal details with terms highlighted
- Sub2 Owners Club (Skool community) — paid community, pre-qualified buyers actively seeking deals
- Creative Real Estate Facebook Groups — search "creative finance deals", "seller finance deals", "subject to real estate"
- BiggerPockets.com — post in marketplace forum; use "seller financing" and "creative terms" in headline
- Connected Investors marketplace — purpose-built for off-market and creative deals
- PropStream, DealMachine — find and match with investors actively seeking creative deals

**Listing Platforms:**
- LoopNet (commercial/investment properties)
- Crexi.com — investment property marketplace, supports creative term listings
- Homie / Roofstock Marketplace (subject-to / turnkey rentals)
- Land.com / LandWatch (land with owner finance)
- Owner.com — specifically built for owner financing
- Note Investor forums (when selling the note itself)

**Social Media:**
- Facebook Marketplace — list with "seller financing available" prominently in title
- Instagram: Post to local real estate investor groups; use hashtags #sellerfinance #subjectto #creativerealestate #ownerfinance #realestateinvesting
- TikTok/YouTube Shorts: Quick 15-30 second video explaining terms — massive reach with investors
- LinkedIn: Post to real estate investing groups and professionals

### B. Local Real Estate Investor Networks (Direct Access to Active Buyers)

- **REIA meetings** — attend and present the deal; rooms full of sub2 and seller finance buyers. Search Meetup.com and REIA.com for local chapters
- **Meetup.com** — search "real estate investors [your city]"; most active investor groups meet monthly
- **Mastermind groups** — local investor masterminds often have members actively seeking creative deals
- **Wholesaler networks** — local wholesalers know cash buyers who do creative deals; offer referral/co-wholesale fee
- **Hard money lender networks** — HML offices know cash buyers who also do creative deals

### C. Direct Investor Outreach (High-Touch, High-Converting)

Generate outreach list of buyer types:
- **Buy-and-hold landlords** in same zip code (likely own similar properties, want more)
- **Fix-and-flip investors transitioning to holding** (want seller finance for buy-and-hold)
- **Self-directed IRA investors** — perfect for seller finance notes (tax-advantaged returns)
- **Find them via**: county records (LLCs owning multiple properties), PropStream absentee owner lists, local REIA membership lists, real estate attorney referrals

### D. MLS & Traditional Channels (Modified Approach)

- List on MLS with "Seller Financing Available" in headline and remarks
- Add creative terms to private agent remarks for buyer's agents
- Email blast to buyer's agent network: "Seller Financing Available — Great for clients who can't qualify traditionally"
- Use MLS to attract buyer's agents with frustrated buyers (can't get bank loan)

### E. Content Marketing (Build Authority + Inbound Leads)

- Blog post: "Buy This Home With No Bank Qualifying — Seller Financing Available at [address]"
- YouTube video walkthrough of property + terms explanation
- Email your database: "Special opportunity — seller financing available on [address]"
- Direct mail to nearby investor-owned properties

## STEP 4: READY-TO-USE MARKETING COPY

All templates below auto-populate agent name, brokerage, market, and community affiliations from preferences.

### Social Media Post (Facebook Groups / Instagram)
Punchy, investor-friendly language. Lead with terms, not address.

> 🏠 SELLER FINANCING AVAILABLE — [City, State]
> 💰 Price: $[X] | Down: $[X] | Rate: X% | Payment: $X/mo | Balloon: X yrs
> 📍 [Beds/Baths/Sqft] — [Condition]
> ✅ No bank qualifying | ✅ As-is | ✅ Cash flow positive day 1
> DM for details or call [Agent Name] at [Phone] — [Brokerage]

### BiggerPockets Marketplace Post
More detailed, professional. Include: full terms, property specs, neighborhood context, investor return analysis (cap rate, cash-on-cash, cash flow estimate). Signed with agent name and brokerage.

### Email Blast to Buyer's Agent Network
Subject: "Seller Financing Available — Great for Clients Who Can't Qualify Traditionally"

Body: Brief property overview, terms, who this works for, call to action. Auto-signed with agent name, brokerage, market, and any community affiliations from preferences.

### MLS Remarks
Public remarks: highlight terms prominently. Private remarks: give buyer's agents exact terms and explain how their buyer can qualify.

### REIA Pitch (30-Second Elevator Pitch)
A concise verbal pitch for investor meetings: terms + condition + opportunity + call to action.

Example: "I'm [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I have a $250K property in [Market] with seller financing available: $30K down, 5% interest, 30-year amortization, no balloon. It needs cosmetic updates and will cash flow $450/month. Who here wants to talk?"

## STEP 5: INVESTOR OUTREACH SCRIPTS

**Cold Text to Local Investor:**
"Hey [Name], I'm [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I have a seller finance deal in [Market] — $X down, X% rate, $X/mo. Cash flows $X after payment. Would you be open to a 5-minute call?"

**Follow-Up Email After REIA Presentation:**
"Thanks for meeting me at REIA last night. I mentioned the [address] seller finance deal. Attached are the full terms. Let me know if you'd like to schedule a property walkthrough. — [Agent Name], [Brokerage]"

**Response to Inbound Inquiry:**
"Great to hear you're interested! Quick qualification questions: Are you looking to owner-occupy or hold as an investment? How much capital do you have available for down payment? When would you want to close?"

---

## STEP 6: DEAL POSITIONING STRATEGY

Help the agent frame the deal to maximize appeal:

- **If property needs work**: Position for fix-and-hold investors; emphasize below-market payment that still allows profit after rehab
- **If turnkey**: Position for passive income investors; calculate the cap rate and cash-on-cash return for them
- **If in hot appreciation zip code**: Position as appreciation play + current cash flow hybrid opportunity
- **If seller finance with balloon**: Explain the refinance exit strategy for the buyer (build equity, improve credit, refinance into bank loan when ready)
