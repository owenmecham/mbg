## STEP 1: FARM ASSESSMENT

Before planning, assess the opportunity and your current position in the farm.

---

### Turnover Rate Analysis

**Formula:** Estimated homes sold per year = Farm size × turnover rate

| Market Type | Typical Annual Turnover | Expected Listings/Year ([X] homes) |
|---|---|---|
| High-turnover (urban, young families) | 8–10% | [X × 0.09] |
| Normal suburban | 5–7% | [X × 0.06] |
| Stable / low-turnover (older owners, long tenure) | 3–5% | [X × 0.04] |

**Your farm ([X] homes):**
At a typical [X]% turnover rate, expect approximately **[Y] homes** to sell in this neighborhood per year.

At your current market share of [X]%, you are capturing approximately [Z] listings per year from this farm.

**Revenue potential:** If your average commission per listing is $[X], and you capture [Z] listings per year, this farm generates approximately **$[X]/year in GCI from listings alone** — not counting buyer sides from referrals within the neighborhood.

**The math on monthly spend:** At $[budget]/month ($[annual budget]/year), your cost-per-listing is approximately $[annual budget ÷ estimated listings captured].

*Note: Farming is a long game. Most agents see meaningful ROI at 12–18 months. Agents who quit at month 6 never find out if it would have worked.*

---

### Competition Assessment

**Ask the agent (or have them check MLS):**
- How many different agents listed homes in this neighborhood in the past 12 months?
- Is there one agent with dominant market share (30%+ of listings)? Who?
- How are competing agents marketing? (Direct mail, social, yard signs, bus benches — do they have a visible brand in the neighborhood?)

**Positioning based on competition:**

| Situation | Your Strategy |
|---|---|
| No dominant agent — open market | Establish yourself as THE neighborhood specialist immediately. Claim the market before someone else does. |
| One dominant agent (30–50% share) | Don't fight directly — differentiate. Better content, more personal, community-first approach. Be patient. |
| Dominant agent (50%+ share) | Assess carefully. This farm may require 18–24 months to crack. Consider whether a secondary farm makes more sense. |
| You are the dominant agent | Defend your position. Maintain visibility and make it costly for challengers to enter. |

## STEP 5: DOOR-KNOCK STRATEGY

Door-knocking accelerates relationship-building when done correctly. Done poorly, it's awkward and counterproductive. Use these scripts.

**When to door-knock:**
- Just listed or just sold event in the farm (highest relevance, highest conversion)
- Quarterly presence campaign (brief, low-pressure, relationship-building)
- After sending a direct mail piece (put a face to the name)
- Open house in the farm (invite neighbors directly)

**What to bring:** Business cards, a leave-behind flyer (market update or just sold piece), a notepad to capture contact info, and a calm, unhurried energy.

**Rules:**
- Always respect No Soliciting signs — do not knock on those doors
- Check local ordinances for any permit requirements
- Go between 10 AM–12 PM or 4–6 PM on weekdays; 10 AM–5 PM on weekends
- If no answer: leave a door hanger or hand-addressed note, not a generic flyer

---

### Script 1: Just Listed / Just Sold Knock (highest conversion)

"Hi — sorry to bother you. I'm [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I just [listed/sold] [nearby address] and I wanted to introduce myself to the neighborhood.

[If just sold]: That home sold in [X days] for $[X] — and honestly, a lot of people in the neighborhood are curious about what their home would sell for right now. I have a couple of buyers who were interested in that home and are still looking.

Is there any chance you're thinking about selling, or do you know anyone nearby who might be?

[If no]: No problem at all. Would it be helpful if I sent you a quick market update showing what homes are selling for in [Neighborhood] right now? I put one out monthly and most neighbors find it genuinely useful. Can I grab your email?"

---

### Script 2: Quarterly Presence Knock (relationship building)

"Hi! I'm [Agent Name] — I've been sending the [Neighborhood] market updates for the past [X months]. I wanted to pop by to put a face to the name and make sure you've been getting them.

[If yes]: Great — glad they're useful. Have you had any questions about what's going on in the market?

[If no]: That's okay — I may have the wrong mailing address. Can I confirm it? [Get address — update records.] And what's your email? I can also send it digitally.

Either way — it's great to meet you. I'm always just a call or text away if you ever have real estate questions. Here's my card."

---

### Script 3: Open House Neighbor Invite

"Hi — I'm [Agent Name]. I'm hosting an open house at [address] [this Saturday from 1–3 PM]. I wanted to personally invite you to come see the inside of the home — a lot of neighbors like to take a peek, and you might know someone who'd love to live on this street.

Also — and I ask everyone I meet here — have you ever thought about selling? Homes like [address] are selling for around $[X] right now. Just something to know."

---

### Handling Common Responses

**"We're not interested in selling."**
"Completely understand — I'm not trying to pressure anyone. I just like knowing the neighbors when I'm working in the area. Here's my card in case anything ever comes up or you hear of someone looking."

**"We already have an agent."**
"Perfect — good to know you're taken care of. What I do is send neighborhood market updates to everyone on the street regardless of who their agent is. You deserve to know what your home is worth. Can I add you to my list? No sales pitch, just data."

**"What does the market look like right now?"**
This is your opening. Spend 2–3 minutes sharing what you know. Offer to send a full analysis. Get their contact info.

## STEP 6: COMMUNITY PRESENCE STRATEGY

Direct mail gets you recognized. Community presence earns trust. Both are required for long-term farm dominance.

---

**Quarterly Event Sponsorship / Participation:**
- Identify 1–2 neighborhood or community events per year to sponsor or volunteer at (neighborhood association meetings, school fundraisers, community clean-up days, seasonal block events)
- Sponsorship doesn't require a sales pitch — your name and logo on materials is sufficient
- Follow up after the event with a brief mailer: "It was great seeing so many [Neighborhood] neighbors at [event] — here's a quick update on the real estate market."

**NextDoor / Facebook Neighborhood Groups:**
- Join the [Neighborhood] NextDoor and any local Facebook groups
- Share genuine, helpful content: market updates (without hard selling), safety tips, local business recommendations, event announcements
- Respond to real estate questions helpfully — do not pitch
- Avoid: posting listings repeatedly, promotional content, anything that feels like an advertisement
- *Over time:* being the helpful, knowledgeable voice in the neighborhood group is worth more than any postcard

**Local Business Partnership:**
- Partner with 1–2 local businesses for cross-promotion
  - Option A: Co-brand a seasonal mailer ("Presented by [Agent Name] and [Business Name]")
  - Option B: Leave your market update card at their counter
  - Option C: Feature their business in your community spotlight mailer
- Choose businesses that homeowners in your farm already love: neighborhood coffee shop, local gym, school-adjacent business, popular restaurant

**Quarterly Neighborhood Blog Post / Video:**
- Write or record one short piece of neighborhood-specific content per quarter
  - "What $600k buys you in [Neighborhood] right now"
  - "The 5 things buyers always ask about [Neighborhood]"
  - "[Neighborhood] schools — what the data shows"
  - "The best blocks in [Neighborhood] (my honest take)"
- Post to your website, share to your social channels, and include in your email signature as a resource
